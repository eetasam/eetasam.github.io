OVERVIEW
Case Study: MyGrocery is a mobile application featuring an in-store shopping list, sorted by aisles, so that customers can optimize their shopping experiences and reduce time spent navigating the store.
AUDIENCE
This app is built for 18 to 50 year olds in the United States with varying incomes who frequent local grocery stores. The target audience is particularly tech-savvy, own smartphones, and utilize non-traditional methods of grocery browsing and shopping.
PROBLEM
A New Grocery App
A new client is creating a grocery store app for the local grocery store. They have asked for initial research to help determine specific problems, or pain points, users have with grocery shopping that their new app might be able to solve.
INITIAL RESEARCH
After interviewing grocery store customers, a few pain points were revealed:
Three main issues were apparent in the grocery store experience:
  1. Interviewees were frustrated that they cannot find every item they need in one store, but they are willing to shop around if they have to.
  2. Interviewees were frustrated by long lines in and outside of the store, as well as difficult checkout procedures in stores.
  3. Interviewees have a tough time keeping track of their shopping list as they traverse the aisles, and waste time trying to find the items they need.
Link to Interview Guide
SOLUTION
My goal was to develop an app for a local grocery store that featured 3 main elements:
1. In Store Shopping List
The app has an in-store shopping list, sorted by aisles, so that customers can find items quickly and reduce time spent navigating the store.
2. Shopping Route Generation
The app presents the user with an optimized shopping route through the store, organized by the user's shopping list.
3. Automatic Coupons and Rewards
The app allows users to browse deals through their shopping lists and provides rewards for frequent shoppers.
